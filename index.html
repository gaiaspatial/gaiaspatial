Gaia Spatial offers unique capabilities in translating environmental questions to geospatial analysis, maximizing successful selection and implementation of optimal concepts, tools and data.

Our approach to geospatial analysis is an iterative process of translation between real world phenomena and simplified representations in order to transform data to information.

With our interdisciplinary background and cross-disciplinary liaising skills, we bring integrative perspectives to our partnerships in transforming information to insight.

Ecosystems comprise complex biotic and abiotic nonlinear dynamics, which are most coherently appraised within multi-scalar, multi-dimensional, network and site specific, socio-biophysical context.

Framing socio-ecological questions into landscape context advances multi-scalar integrative perspectives.

Whether the questions involve dynamics in socio-environmental planning, plant or wildlife distributions, forest health, water quality/quantity or all encompassing climate change, connections between life forms and the resources and habitat they utilize are manifested across landscapes in space and time.
